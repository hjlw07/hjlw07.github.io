Building a Future of Healthcare

State of Hawaii alone has a shortage of 700 physcians right now. Shortage in healthcare does not only end in Hawaiʻi but also the rest of the Pacific, from Samoa to Saipan.

There are multiple levels of training for the medical pathway. You can start working with a certificate, then pursue an associateʻs degree, move on to a bachelorʻs degree, and even end upgetting a masterʻs, doctoral, or other professional degree.

Academic Degree

  • Certificate — takes weeks up to 1 year of college education
  • Associate Degree — 2-3 years of college education
  • Bachelor Degree — 4+ years of college education
  • Master Degree — Additional 2+ years of school after bachelor program; total of 6 years of college education
  • Doctoral Degree - Additional 3-7+ years of school after bachelor program; total of 7-11+ years of college education

Financial Aid

  • Schloarships - Free money for school earned through grades, activities, service, or work; usually requires an application
  • Grants - Free financial aid based on student's financial need, determinded by FAFFSA
  • Loans - Money borrowed from the school but must be paid back, subsidized loans: interest starts after repayment begins, unsubsidized loans: interest starts immediately
  • Work Study - Helps students with financial need earn money for school, provides tax-free income, work part-time while attending school
